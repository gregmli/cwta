Chen Style Taichi Classes

Chen Style Taichi for Beginners

Sunday 4:00pm - 5:00pm (at Mercer Island, starting October 1st, 2017)
Monday 6:30pm - 7:30pm (starting March 20th, 2017)
Instructor: Master Yijiao Hong

Master Yijiao Hong will cover authentic Chen Taichi Silk Reeling and Qigong warm-ups from Traditional Chinese Medicine, giving in-depth, step-by-step instruction and using the latest in physical learning science. Master Hong will also cover how Taichi originated from ancient Chinese philosophy and Chinese medicine. This class will emphasize the skills of self-centering, body relaxation, and slow breathing as you practice form movements. You can expect increased flexibility, leg strength, coordination, and balance, as well as learning to be relaxed in an energetic way.

Taichi is an ideal aerobic exercise for life time. Do you know that practicing Taichi maintains spine health and increases bone density? It is beneficial to the cardio-vascular, respiratory, and nervous systems, digestive system, balance and also improves circulation of Chi. Master Hong's long-term Taichi students report that practicing Taichi helps alleviate hypertension, asthma, knee pain, and stress, while also improving mental, body strength and overall happiness.

Sunday afternoon class will be held at the CWTA Mercer Island (2827 80th Ave SE, Mercer Island)

Tuition: $190, plus $50 for t-shirt and registration

Quarter Schedule

CWTA operates on a quarterly schedule, each quarter lasting 10-12 weeks.

  • 2nd quarter: March 19 - June 14 (10 weeks). Spring break April 2nd to 19th. Snow days make-up: April 3rd and 4th
  • 3rd quarter: July 16 - September 23 (10 weeeks)

Registration & Payment

If you are new, submit a registration form to register now! Add $50 for uniform and registration fee.

Payment is due before the end of the previous quarter. Payments received after the due date are subject to a $20 late fee.

Please make checks payable to CWTA LLC.

Please note: no refund / mininum one quarter commitment

Makeup Policy

Sometimes students will miss one or more classes due to an out of town trip, sickness etc. Students can make up classes by attending any similar class that occurs on another day. Please check with instructor to see what classes are available to use as make up classes. Also, instructors are available 10 minutes Before and after class to work with you on the new movements when you return to class.
